We got good at saving notes. Terrible at finding them again.

I've been exploring how AI can fix this, and three very different approaches are emerging in 2026:

1. Obsidian + AI plugins — bolt an LLM onto your existing notes
2. Claude Code AutoMemory — the AI builds its own memory automatically, then "dreams" to consolidate it between sessions
3. Andrej Karpathy's approach — let the LLM write and maintain the entire wiki. You barely touch the markdown yourself.

The surprising finding from Karpathy's setup: at ~400K words, structured markdown with clean index files beat fancy RAG pipelines. Sometimes the simple approach wins.

A few quick tips that came out of the research:

→ Claude Code's CLAUDE.md caps at 200 lines — anything longer loses weight as the conversation grows
→ If AI can't quote the specific note, it shouldn't claim the info exists. One rule that kills most hallucination risk.
→ Keep your personal vault separate from your AI vault. Otherwise you end up with ideas you can't source.
